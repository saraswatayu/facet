## Options to Test

### Model A: $30/month flat (status quo)

The current pricing. One plan, one price. Every user gets every feature. No free tier, no trials beyond the onboarding session.

Key dynamics:
- Maximum revenue per user — no discount tier cannibalizing the core
- The price itself is a positioning signal ("this is premium email for serious professionals")
- Creates a hard conversion barrier — users must commit $30/month before experiencing the product daily
- Simplicity: no tier confusion, no upsell friction, no feature gating
- The onboarding session partially substitutes for a free trial (users see the product in action)

### Model B: Free tier + $30/month Pro (freemium)

A free tier with core speed and keyboard shortcuts, but AI features (auto-triage, draft replies, thread summaries, tone adjustment) gated behind the $30/month Pro plan.

Free tier includes: Split Inbox, keyboard shortcuts, Undo Send, basic search, mobile apps.
Pro tier adds: AI triage, AI writing, AI summaries, Send Later, Read Statuses, Snippets, Follow-Up Reminders, team features.

Key dynamics:
- Massively expands top-of-funnel (free removes the biggest objection)
- AI features are the natural upsell — users experience the speed, then want the intelligence
- Risk: free users may be "good enough" and never convert (the Gmail-is-free problem)
- Risk: dilutes the premium brand positioning — Superhuman becomes "another email app"
- Free users create word-of-mouth but generate zero revenue unless they convert

### Model C: $15/month Starter + $30/month Pro (two paid tiers)

Starter ($15/month): Core speed, keyboard shortcuts, Split Inbox, Undo Send, Send Later, mobile apps.
Pro ($30/month): Everything in Starter + AI features, Read Statuses, Snippets, Follow-Up Reminders, team features, priority support.

Key dynamics:
- Lower entry point captures price-sensitive professionals who can't justify $30
- Clear upgrade path as users discover AI features
- The $15 tier validates willingness to pay before the $30 ask
- Risk: most current $30 users downgrade to $15 (revenue cannibalization)
- Risk: two tiers add complexity to onboarding, marketing, and support
- The $15/$30 spread may not feel meaningful enough — $15/month for email still feels expensive

### Crossover Analysis

- Model A maximizes revenue per user but limits addressable market
- Model B maximizes users but risks revenue if conversion rate is low (industry freemium conversion: 2-5%)
- Model C is the compromise — lower barrier than A, more revenue than B — but risks cannibalizing A

At 100K users:
- Model A at 100% paying: $3M/month
- Model B at 4% conversion: needs 2.5M free users to match Model A revenue
- Model C at 60% Starter / 40% Pro: $2.1M/month (30% revenue haircut vs Model A)
