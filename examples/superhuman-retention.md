## Strategies to Test

### Strategy A: Status Quo (product-led retention)

No active retention mechanics beyond the core product. Users stay because Superhuman is fast, useful, and habitual. No usage emails, no milestone badges, no "we miss you" campaigns. The product experience IS the retention strategy.

Key dynamics:
- Respects user attention — no notification fatigue
- Relies entirely on product quality and habit formation
- Users who drift away get no nudge to return
- Churn is invisible until the cancellation event
- Aligns with Superhuman's premium, non-needy brand identity
- Risk: hedonic adaptation erodes excitement with no mechanism to counteract it

### Strategy B: Proactive Engagement

Active lifecycle communications:
- **Weekly digest** (email): "This week you processed 342 emails, replied to 89, and used 14 keyboard shortcuts. You're in the top 20% of Superhuman users."
- **Milestone celebrations**: "You've hit Inbox Zero 50 times!" / "You've been a Superhuman user for 6 months."
- **Feature discovery nudges**: "You haven't tried AI Summarize yet — here's how it works in 30 seconds."
- **Re-engagement triggers**: If usage drops >50% week-over-week, send "Your inbox has 47 unread emails. Catch up in Superhuman →"
- **Community**: Access to a private Slack/Discord with productivity tips, workflows, and direct feedback channel

Key dynamics:
- Keeps Superhuman top-of-mind during low-engagement periods
- Gamification elements (stats, rankings) appeal to competitive personas
- Feature discovery extends the product's perceived depth
- Risk: notification fatigue — weekly emails feel promotional, not valuable
- Risk: usage stats can backfire ("I processed 342 emails" → "Why am I getting so much email?")
- Risk: re-engagement nudges feel guilt-trippy to some personas

### Strategy C: Value Reinforcement (ROI reports)

Monthly "Superhuman Impact Report" delivered via email and in-app:
- **Time saved**: "You saved an estimated 14.2 hours this month using Superhuman" (calculated from keyboard shortcut usage, AI features, Split Inbox time savings)
- **Dollar value**: "At your estimated hourly rate, that's $1,420 in recovered productivity"
- **Comparison**: "Without Superhuman, your average email response time would be ~4.2 minutes. With Superhuman, it's ~1.1 minutes."
- **Trend line**: 3-month trajectory showing whether they're getting MORE or LESS value over time
- **Renewal context**: As the billing anniversary approaches, the report adds: "Over the past year, Superhuman saved you an estimated 168 hours — worth $16,800 at your rate. Your subscription cost: $360."

Key dynamics:
- Directly combats the "is this worth $30/month?" question with concrete numbers
- The ROI frame reanchors the price against value, not against free alternatives
- Monthly cadence is low-frequency enough to avoid fatigue
- The dollar value calculation makes the abstract ("I'm faster") concrete ("I saved $1,420")
- Risk: if the numbers are low ("you saved 2 hours this month"), it undermines the case
- Risk: estimated savings feel made up — "how do you know I saved 14 hours?"
- Risk: personas who don't think in ROI terms (creatives, non-business users) may find this irrelevant or crass
