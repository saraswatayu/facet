# Product: Superhuman

Superhuman is a premium email client built for speed. It costs $30/month — in a world where Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail are free. The core promise is "the fastest email experience ever made": every interaction completes in under 100ms, the entire app is keyboard-driven, and AI features handle triage, writing, and summarization.

Users report saving 3-4 hours per week on email. The product has a famously selective onboarding process (1-on-1 concierge session) and a long waitlist history, though it has since opened up.

## Key Product Details

- $30/month per user (individual plan)
- $30/month per user (team plan, 5+ users, adds shared conversations, team insights, read statuses)
- Works with Gmail and Outlook accounts only (not a standalone email provider)
- AI features: auto-summarize threads, draft replies, instant triage ("important" vs "not important"), tone adjustment
- Keyboard-first design: every action has a shortcut, most users never touch the mouse
- Split Inbox: automatic categorization into feeds (newsletters, notifications, VIP, etc.)
- Snippets: templated responses with dynamic variables
- Send Later, Undo Send, Read Statuses, Follow-Up Reminders
- Available on macOS, Windows, iOS, Android
- No free tier, no freemium — $30/month or nothing
- 1-on-1 onboarding session included with every new account

## Target Market

Professionals who spend 2+ hours per day in email and whose productivity directly impacts their income. Key segments to consider:
- Startup founders and CEOs (email is their primary communication tool)
- Sales professionals (email velocity directly impacts quota)
- Venture capitalists and investors (hundreds of inbound emails daily)
- Executive assistants (managing multiple inboxes)
- Consultants and professional services (client communication)
- Product managers (cross-functional coordination)
- Recruiters (high-volume candidate outreach)
- Lawyers (time-sensitive, high-stakes communication)
- Freelancers and solopreneurs (wearing every hat, time is scarce)
- People who've tried every productivity app and are still drowning in email
