## What's Being Tested

Which positioning angle works best for Superhuman's $30/month price point? All variants describe the same product at the same price, but frame the value differently — speed, productivity ROI, AI intelligence, social proof, and anti-free contrarianism — to see which framing resonates across segments.

The core challenge: convincing people to pay $30/month for email when Gmail is free.

## Copy Variants

### Variant A: "Speed Purity"
Every interaction under 100ms. Every action has a shortcut.
Email at the speed of thought.

$30/month.

CTA: "Try Superhuman"

### Variant B: "Time is Money"
The average Superhuman user saves 4 hours every week.
At $50/hour, that's $10,000/year in recovered productivity.
$30/month. 26:1 return on a tool you'll use every day.

CTA: "Start Saving Time"

### Variant C: "AI Brain for Your Inbox"
AI that reads your email before you do.
Instant thread summaries. Draft replies in your voice. Auto-triage what matters.
Your inbox, managed by intelligence. $30/month.

CTA: "Get Superhuman AI"

### Variant D: "Social Proof, Exclusive"
The email client used by founders at Stripe, Airbnb, and Figma.
75% of YC founders use Superhuman.
$30/month. Join the waitlist.

CTA: "Join the Waitlist"

### Variant E: "Anti-Free Contrarian"
Gmail is free. Your time isn't.
The most expensive email client is the one that wastes your morning.
Superhuman costs $30/month because it saves you 4 hours a week.

CTA: "Reclaim Your Inbox"

### Variant F: "Calm Control"
Inbox Zero isn't a goal. It's a starting point.
Split Inbox sorts your email automatically. AI handles the rest.
Less noise. More focus. $30/month.

CTA: "Take Control"
